Tools & Software I Use

I use a mix of computational programs, post-processing tools, and visualization software to explore nanoscale systems.

  • DFT Packages I Frequently Use:
    Gaussian, PySCF, DMol3
    Used for non-periodic systems, structural optimization, spin-resolved properties, and wavefunction analysis in nanoflakes and quantum dots.
  • Other conventional programs that I have worked with:
    VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO
    Primarily adopted for studying periodic structures and their non-colinear spin texture.
  • Quantum Transport Simulations:
    TranSIESTA, NEGF formalism
    Used to investigate charge/spin transport in nanoelectronic and spintronic devices. NEGF-based tools help model current–voltage characteristics under quantum coherent conditions.
  • Visualization & Data Analysis:
    GaussView, Multiwfn, VESTA, Pymatgen, GaussSum
    For visualizing molecular orbitals, DOS, spin densities, charge densities, and crystal structures.
  • Programming & Scripting:
    Python (NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib, ASE), Julia
    Used for data extraction, plotting, and scripting parameter sweeps.
  • Other tools I find useful:
    KWANT: For quantum transport calculations with tight-binding approach
    VAMPIRE: For atomistic spin dynamics with Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert, and Metropolis (MC) algorithm
