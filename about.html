About Me

I am Saraf Mohaimen Chowdhury, an early-career researcher passionate about quantum simulations, 2D materials, and nanoelectronic devices. My work blends first-principles theory with cutting-edge computational tools to explore spintronics, magnetism, and quantum transport at the nanoscale.

Books That Influenced Me

  • Principles of Condensed Matter Physics – Chaikin & Lubensky
    Shaped my understanding of collective behavior in low-dimensional systems.
  • Quantum Field Theory of Many-Body Systems – Xiao-Gang Wen
    Influenced my approach to emergent quantum phenomena.
  • Introduction to Solid State Physics – Charles Kittel
    Provided the foundation for my interest in electronic structure and crystallography.

Literatures That Shaped My Thinking

  • Novoselov et al., Electric Field Effect in Atomically Thin Carbon Films, Science (2004)
    Introduced me to the fascinating world of graphene and 2D materials.
  • Zunger, Practical DFT and Beyond, Phys. Rev. (2010)
    Helped me adopt DFT as a practical tool for predictive material science.
  • Datta, Electronic Transport in Mesoscopic Systems
    Guided my understanding of quantum transport and NEGF formalism.
