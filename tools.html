Tools & Software I Use

My research integrates a wide range of computational tools spanning density functional theory (DFT), molecular dynamics, quantum transport, and machine learning. Below are the primary programs and environments I use regularly, along with their application areas:

  • DFT Packages:
    Gaussian, Quantum ESPRESSO, PySCF, SIESTA, BigDFT
    Used for ground-state calculations, structural optimization, spin-resolved properties, and wavefunction analysis in 2D materials and quantum dots.
  • Molecular Dynamics:
    LAMMPS, ReaxFF
    Employed for studying thermal stability, atomistic behavior, and surface processes; ReaxFF is particularly useful for reactive simulations.
  • Quantum Transport Simulations:
    TranSIESTA, NEGF formalism, NEMO5
    Used to investigate charge/spin transport in nanoelectronic and spintronic devices. NEGF-based tools help model currentâ€“voltage characteristics under quantum coherent conditions.
  • Visualization & Data Analysis:
    GaussView, VESTA, Avogadro
    For visualizing molecular orbitals, spin densities, charge densities, and crystal structures.
  • Programming & Scripting:
    Python (NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib, ASE), MATLAB
    Used for automating simulations, data extraction, plotting, and scripting parameter sweeps.
  • Machine Learning Frameworks:
    PyTorch
    Currently used for modeling soliton dynamics, PDE solvers, and physics-informed neural networks using bilinear transformations.
